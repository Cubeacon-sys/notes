\section{Solving Logic Puzzles}
\subsection{Knight and Knave}
An island has two kinds of inhabitants, \textit{knights}, who always tell the truth, and \textit{knaves} who always lie. You go to the island and meet $ A $ and $ B $, $ A $ says ``$ B $ is a \textit{knight}``. $ B $ says ``the two of us are \textbf{opposite} types``. What are $ A $ and $ B $? Let $ p $ represent that $ A $ is a knight and $ q $ represent that $ B $ is a knight.\\
$ p $: $ A $ is a \textit{knight}. \\
$ q $: $ B $ is a \textit{knight}. \\


There are 4 possibilities: 
\begin{itemize}
	\item False: $ p \land q $ $ A $ is a \textit{knight}, $ B $ is a \textit{knight}.
	\item False: $ p \land \neg q $ $ A $ is a \textit{knight}, $ B $ is a \textit{knave}.
	\item False: $ \neg p \land q $ $ A $ is a \textit{knave}, $ B $ is a \textit{knight}.
	\item \textbf{True}: $ \neg p \land \neg q $ $ A $ is a \textit{knave}, $ B $ is a \textit{knave}.
\end{itemize}

In table form it is: \\
\begin{center}
$ p $: $ A $ is a \textit{knight} $ q $: $ B $ is a \textit{knight}
\end{center}
